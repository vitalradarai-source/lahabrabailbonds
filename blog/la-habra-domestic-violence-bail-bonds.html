La Habra Domestic Violence Bail Bonds: Arrest, Booking, and Getting Released

La Habra sits at the northern tip of Orange County, sharing a border with Los Angeles County. That geography matters when a domestic violence arrest occurs — it determines which agency responds, where the defendant is booked, and which courthouse handles the case. This guide covers all of it so families know exactly what steps to take.

What Happens at a Domestic Violence Arrest in La Habra

La Habra has its own police department — the La Habra Police Department (LHPD) — which handles all DV calls within city limits. La Habra PD operates under Orange County jurisdiction, so Orange County bail schedules and court procedures apply regardless of how close the incident is to the LA County line.

Under California Penal Code § 836(c), LHPD officers must arrest when they have probable cause that domestic violence occurred. The alleged victim's willingness to cooperate does not determine whether an arrest is made. When LHPD responds to a DV call, officers will:

  • Separate the parties and take statements individually
  • Photograph evidence and document the scene
  • Identify the primary aggressor based on physical evidence and statements
  • Issue an Emergency Protective Order (EPO) at the scene
  • Transport the defendant to La Habra PD's holding area for initial processing

In rare situations where an incident straddles the La Habra / LA County boundary, jurisdiction may shift. When LHPD makes the arrest, however, the case proceeds through Orange County — including bail, booking, and arraignment.

Booking at Theo Lacy Facility in Orange

After initial processing at La Habra PD, defendants facing domestic violence charges are transferred to Theo Lacy Facility (320 N. Flower St., Orange, CA 92868) for full booking. Theo Lacy is the OCSD's primary North Orange County booking facility and handles arrests from La Habra, Brea, Placentia, Anaheim, and Fullerton.

The complete booking process at Theo Lacy typically takes 2 to 6 hours from the time the defendant arrives. The steps include:

  • Fingerprinting, photographing, and full identity confirmation
  • Warrant check through statewide and national databases
  • Charge entry and bail assignment per the Orange County schedule
  • Classification and placement until bail is posted

How to Post Bail for a La Habra Domestic Violence Arrest

Paying full cash bail is rarely practical. A licensed bail bondsman posts the entire bail amount with the facility in exchange for a 10% premium — the state-mandated fee under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. On a $50,000 bail, the cost to the family is $5,000. This fee is earned and non-refundable when the bond is posted.

Protective Orders and Bail — Key Facts for La Habra Cases

An Emergency Protective Order (EPO) is automatically issued by LHPD officers at the scene of a DV arrest. This order goes into effect immediately and prohibits the defendant from contacting or approaching the protected person. It does not, however, prevent the defendant from posting bail and being released from custody.

The EPO is a civil restraining order. Bail is part of the criminal process. They are parallel systems that operate independently. A defendant arrested in La Habra can be home the same day as the arrest, even with an EPO in place — as long as they comply with its terms after release.

The arraignment takes place at the North Justice Center (1275 N. Berkeley Ave., Fullerton), which handles DV cases from La Habra, Brea, Fullerton, Anaheim, and nearby North OC cities. At arraignment, the judge reviews the EPO, may convert it to a Criminal Protective Order under PC § 136.2, and will formally address bail. The bail bond posted after booking remains in effect through the arraignment and beyond unless the court orders otherwise.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does booking take after a La Habra DV arrest?

Booking at Theo Lacy typically takes 2 to 6 hours from the time the defendant arrives at the facility. There is also transfer time from La Habra PD to Theo Lacy. Calling Angels Bail Bonds as early as possible — even before the defendant leaves La Habra PD — lets us prepare the bond paperwork in advance so we can post immediately after booking clears.

Can bail be posted right after a domestic violence arrest in La Habra?

Yes. As soon as the booking process is complete and bail is assigned in the system, a bail bond can be posted. There is no mandatory waiting period following a domestic violence arrest in California. Release typically follows within a few hours of the bond being posted at Theo Lacy.

What does the 10% bail bond premium cover?

The 10% premium is the licensed bail bondsman's fee for posting the full bail amount with the jail. It is required by California state law and is non-refundable once the bond is posted — even if charges are later dropped or the defendant is acquitted. For a $50,000 PC § 273.5 bail, the premium is $5,000. Angels Bail Bonds offers flexible payment arrangements for families who qualify.

Does it matter that La Habra borders Los Angeles County?

For most arrests in La Habra, no. When La Habra Police Department makes the arrest, the case is handled entirely within Orange County — including booking at Theo Lacy and arraignment at the North Justice Center in Fullerton. Orange County bail amounts apply. Only if an incident literally crosses into LA County and an LA County agency makes the arrest would LA County procedures and bail schedules apply.
